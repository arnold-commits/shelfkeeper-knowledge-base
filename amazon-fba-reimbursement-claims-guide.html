⚡ Quick Answer

Amazon owes you reimbursements when they lose, damage, or destroy your inventory, overcharge fees, or fail to reimburse customer returns. Check your Inventory Adjustments and Reimbursements reports regularly. Most sellers recover $1,000-$10,000+ annually in missed reimbursements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of FBA reimbursements can I claim? ▼

You can claim reimbursements for: lost inventory in FBA warehouses, damaged inventory during receiving or fulfillment, customer returns not received back, overcharged FBA fees, weight/dimension measurement errors, and destroyed inventory without authorization.

How do I find missing FBA reimbursements? ▼

Compare your Inventory Adjustments report with your Reimbursements report in Seller Central. Look for adjustments where Amazon deducted inventory but didn't issue a corresponding reimbursement within 30-45 days. Also check the Manage FBA Returns report.

How long do I have to file a reimbursement claim? ▼

Amazon's reimbursement policy allows claims within 18 months of the incident. However, it's best to check monthly and file claims promptly. Older claims may be harder to get approved as Amazon may have less data available.

How much can I recover from FBA reimbursements? ▼

Most active FBA sellers can recover 1-3% of their annual revenue through reimbursement claims. For a seller doing $500K/year, that's $5,000-$15,000 in potential recoveries. Regularly auditing your account is essential.
