Frequently Asked Questions

How does Amazon's payment cycle work? ▼

Amazon pays sellers every 14 days via settlement disbursements. The settlement includes gross sales minus fees, refunds, and reserves. New sellers may have a 7-day reserve holdback. Plan your inventory purchases around these bi-weekly payment cycles.

How do I improve cash flow for my Amazon FBA business? ▼

Improve cash flow by: negotiating better payment terms with suppliers (Net 30-60), using inventory financing or business credit lines, maintaining lean inventory (30-60 days supply), pricing for healthy margins, and reducing slow-moving inventory quickly.

What is a healthy cash reserve for an Amazon FBA business? ▼

Keep 2-3 months of operating expenses as a cash reserve, plus enough for your next inventory purchase. For a business with $10K/month in expenses and $15K inventory costs, aim for $35K-$45K minimum in accessible cash or credit.

Should I use financing for Amazon FBA inventory? ▼

Inventory financing can fuel growth if used strategically. Options include Amazon Lending, Payability, Clearco, or traditional business lines of credit. Only use financing if your profit margins can cover the financing costs (typically 1-4% monthly).
