Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important financial metrics for Amazon FBA? ▼

Top metrics: 1) Net Profit Margin (15-25% target), 2) Gross Margin (40-60%), 3) TACoS (5-15%), 4) Return on Investment per product (100%+), 5) Inventory Turnover Rate (6-12x/year), 6) Cash Conversion Cycle (under 60 days), 7) Sell-Through Rate, 8) Unit Economics per SKU.

How do I calculate ROI for Amazon FBA products? ▼

FBA Product ROI = (Net Profit / Total Investment) x 100. Total Investment includes COGS, shipping to Amazon, prep costs, and initial advertising spend. Example: $1,000 total investment generating $2,500 revenue with $1,500 profit = 150% ROI. Aim for minimum 100% ROI per product.

What is a good inventory turnover rate for Amazon FBA? ▼

A good inventory turnover rate for FBA is 6-12 times per year (turning over every 30-60 days). Calculate: Annual COGS / Average Inventory Value. High turnover means efficient use of capital. Low turnover may indicate overstocking, wrong product selection, or pricing issues.

How often should I review my FBA financial dashboard? ▼

Review key metrics weekly (sales, advertising, margins). Do a comprehensive financial review monthly (P&L, cash flow, inventory). Perform deep analysis quarterly (product-level profitability, channel comparison, year-over-year trends). Use ShelfKeeper for automated real-time tracking.
