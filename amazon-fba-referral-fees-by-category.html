⚡ Quick Answer

Amazon charges referral fees of 8-45% depending on the product category, with most categories at 15%. Categories like Amazon Device Accessories (45%), Clothing (17%), and Electronics (8%) vary significantly. Always factor referral fees into your pricing strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average Amazon referral fee? ▼

The average Amazon referral fee across most categories is 15% of the selling price. However, fees range from 8% (personal computers) to 45% (Amazon device accessories). Some categories have minimum per-item referral fees of $0.30.

How are referral fees calculated? ▼

Referral fees are calculated as a percentage of the total sales price, including the item price and any shipping or gift wrap charges. The fee is the greater of the applicable percentage or the minimum referral fee for that category.

Which product categories have the lowest referral fees? ▼

Categories with the lowest referral fees include: Personal Computers (6%), Video Game Consoles (8%), Consumer Electronics (8%), and Automotive (12%). These lower fees can improve profit margins significantly.

Can I reduce my Amazon referral fees? ▼

You cannot negotiate referral fees, but you can: choose products in lower-fee categories, bundle items to reduce per-unit fee impact, optimize pricing to maintain margins, and consider whether FBM vs FBA makes sense for high-fee categories.
