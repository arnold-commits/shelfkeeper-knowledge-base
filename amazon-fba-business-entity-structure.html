⚡ Quick Answer

Most Amazon FBA sellers should start as an LLC for liability protection, then consider S-Corp election once net income exceeds $40,000-$50,000 annually for self-employment tax savings. An S-Corp can save 15.3% in self-employment taxes on distributions above a reasonable salary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I form an LLC for my Amazon FBA business? ▼

Yes, forming an LLC is recommended for most Amazon FBA sellers. It provides personal liability protection, separating your personal assets from business debts. LLCs are relatively inexpensive to form ($50-$500 depending on the state) and easy to maintain.

When should I elect S-Corp status for my FBA business? ▼

Consider S-Corp election when your net business income consistently exceeds $40,000-$50,000 annually. S-Corp allows you to pay yourself a reasonable salary and take remaining profits as distributions, saving 15.3% self-employment tax on the distribution portion.

What state should I form my LLC in for Amazon FBA? ▼

Generally, form your LLC in the state where you live and operate your business. While Delaware and Wyoming offer advantages for larger companies, the additional costs of registering as a foreign LLC in your home state usually outweigh benefits for small FBA sellers.

Do I need an EIN for my Amazon FBA business? ▼

Yes, get a free EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS even if you have no employees. It's required for LLC and S-Corp entities, helps separate personal and business identity, and is needed for business bank accounts and Amazon seller registration.
