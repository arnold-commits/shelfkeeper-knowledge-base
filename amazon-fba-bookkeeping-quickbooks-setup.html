Frequently Asked Questions

What chart of accounts should I use for Amazon FBA in QuickBooks? ▼

Key accounts include: Income (Amazon Sales, Shipping Income, Other Income), COGS (Product Purchases, Inbound Shipping, Prep Costs), Expenses (FBA Fees, Referral Fees, Storage Fees, Advertising, Software, Shipping Supplies), and Assets (Inventory, Amazon Receivable).

Should I use cash or accrual accounting for Amazon FBA? ▼

Accrual accounting is recommended for FBA sellers doing over $1M or using inventory. It matches revenue with expenses in the same period, giving a more accurate picture. However, smaller sellers can use cash basis, which is simpler. Consult your accountant.

How do I record Amazon settlement payments in QuickBooks? ▼

Record each bi-weekly Amazon settlement as a deposit, breaking it into components: gross sales, refunds, FBA fees, referral fees, advertising charges, and other deductions. Use a connector like A2X for automatic categorization, or enter manually from settlement reports.

How often should I reconcile my Amazon bookkeeping? ▼

Reconcile your Amazon bookkeeping at least monthly. Match Amazon settlement reports with your bank deposits, verify all fees and deductions, and ensure inventory values are accurate. Quarterly, do a deep review of all categories and P&L accuracy.
