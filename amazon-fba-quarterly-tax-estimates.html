⚡ Quick Answer

FBA sellers must pay quarterly estimated taxes if they expect to owe $1,000+ in federal taxes. Payments are due April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15. Calculate by estimating annual income, applying your tax rate (typically 25-35% combined federal and state), and dividing by 4.

Overview

How to calculate and pay quarterly estimated tax payments as an Amazon FBA seller to avoid underpayment penalties.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate quarterly estimated taxes for Amazon FBA? ▼

Estimate your annual taxable income from FBA (revenue minus all deductible expenses and COGS). Apply your effective tax rate (federal + state + self-employment). Divide by 4 for quarterly payments. Alternatively, use the safe harbor method: pay 100% of last year's total tax liability divided by 4.

What happens if I don't pay quarterly estimated taxes? ▼

The IRS charges an underpayment penalty (currently ~8% annual rate) on the amount you should have paid each quarter. Some states also charge penalties. You can avoid penalties by paying at least 90% of current year tax or 100% of prior year tax (110% if income over $150K).

When are quarterly estimated tax payments due? ▼

Federal quarterly estimated tax due dates: Q1 (Jan-Mar) due April 15, Q2 (Apr-May) due June 15, Q3 (Jun-Aug) due September 15, Q4 (Sep-Dec) due January 15 of the following year. State due dates usually match but verify with your state.

How do I pay estimated taxes as an Amazon FBA seller? ▼

Pay federal estimates via IRS Direct Pay (irs.gov/payments), EFTPS (Electronic Federal Tax Payment System), or by mailing Form 1040-ES vouchers. For state taxes, check your state revenue department website. Keep confirmation numbers for all payments.
