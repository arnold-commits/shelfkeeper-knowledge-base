Knowledge Base > Amazon FBA Returns Accounting Guide

⚡ Quick Answer

Account for Amazon FBA returns by recording refunds as contra-revenue (reducing sales, not as an expense), tracking return shipping costs, and monitoring restocking fees collected. Reconcile your returns monthly using the FBA Returns Report and Customer Returns Report in Seller Central.

Overview

How to properly account for Amazon FBA returns, refunds, restocking fees, and return-related expenses in your bookkeeping system.

Frequently Asked Questions

How should I record Amazon returns in my bookkeeping? ▼

Record returns as a deduction from revenue (contra-revenue account), not as an expense. When a $25 item is returned, reduce your sales by $25 and add the item back to inventory. If the item is unsellable, write it off as a loss. This gives accurate gross sales figures.

What is the average return rate for Amazon FBA? ▼

The average Amazon FBA return rate is 5-15% depending on the category. Clothing and shoes have the highest rates (20-30%), while consumables are lower (2-5%). Factor expected returns into your pricing and profitability calculations.

How do restocking fees work on Amazon? ▼

Amazon charges customers a restocking fee in certain cases: 20% for items returned opened in original condition, 50% for items returned in different condition, and up to 100% for certain categories. These fees are credited to sellers and should be recorded as misc income.

How do I reconcile Amazon returns with my inventory? ▼

Monthly, compare the Customer Returns Report with your inventory records. Check if returned items were added back as sellable or unsellable. File reimbursement claims for items Amazon received but didn't add back to inventory. Track return reasons to identify product quality issues.
