Knowledge Base > Amazon FBA Inventory Valuation Methods

⚡ Quick Answer

Amazon FBA sellers should use FIFO (First In, First Out) for inventory valuation, which is the most common and IRS-accepted method. FIFO assumes your oldest inventory costs are matched to sales first. This typically results in lower COGS during inflationary periods and higher taxable income.

Understanding FIFO, LIFO, and weighted average inventory valuation methods for Amazon FBA tax compliance and accurate financial reporting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What inventory valuation method should Amazon FBA sellers use? ▼

FIFO (First In, First Out) is recommended for most Amazon FBA sellers. It's widely accepted by the IRS, easy to implement, and matches the natural flow of inventory in FBA warehouses. Once you choose a method, you must apply it consistently and get IRS approval to change.

How does FIFO work for Amazon FBA inventory? ▼

With FIFO, the cost of your first purchased inventory is assigned to the first units sold. Example: Buy 100 units at $5, then 100 at $6. When you sell 50 units, the COGS is 50 x $5 = $250. Your remaining inventory includes 50 units at $5 and 100 at $6.

What is weighted average inventory valuation? ▼

Weighted average calculates a single average cost per unit after each purchase. Example: 100 units at $5 = $500, then 100 units at $6 = $600. Total = $1,100 / 200 units = $5.50 per unit average cost. This is simpler than FIFO but less common for FBA sellers.

How does inventory valuation affect my taxes? ▼

Inventory valuation directly impacts your COGS and therefore taxable income. FIFO in times of rising costs results in lower COGS and higher taxes. LIFO (not commonly used for FBA) would give higher COGS and lower taxes. Choose the method that best reflects your business reality and consult a CPA.
