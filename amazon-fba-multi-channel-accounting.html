⚡ Quick Answer

For multi-channel FBA accounting, use a centralized accounting system (QuickBooks or Xero) with separate income and expense tracking per channel. Use integration tools like A2X, Webgility, or Link My Books to automate data from each marketplace. Reconcile all channels monthly.

Overview

Managing accounting and bookkeeping across Amazon, Shopify, Walmart, and other sales channels with unified financial reporting.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I track revenue from multiple sales channels? ▼

Create separate income accounts or classes in your accounting software for each channel (Amazon, Shopify, Walmart, etc.). Use marketplace connectors to automatically import and categorize transactions. This allows you to see profitability per channel in your P&L.

What tools help with multi-channel accounting? ▼

Key tools include: A2X (Amazon and Shopify to QuickBooks/Xero), Webgility (multi-channel to QuickBooks), Link My Books (Amazon/Shopify), and Sellerboard (multi-channel profit analytics). Choose based on which channels you sell on and your accounting software.

How do I handle inventory across multiple channels? ▼

Use inventory management software like Sellbrite, ChannelAdvisor, or Linnworks that syncs inventory levels across all channels. In accounting, maintain one inventory account but track channel-specific COGS. MCF (Multi-Channel Fulfillment) uses FBA inventory for non-Amazon orders.

Should I use separate bank accounts for each sales channel? ▼

Having separate bank accounts per channel is ideal for clean accounting but not required. At minimum, have a dedicated business bank account. If using one account, ensure your accounting software properly categorizes deposits by channel for accurate reporting.
