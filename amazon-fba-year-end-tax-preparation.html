⚡ Quick Answer

For year-end tax prep, Amazon FBA sellers should: reconcile all bank and Amazon accounts, finalize inventory count and valuation, gather all receipts and deduction documentation, review and maximize deductions, calculate quarterly estimated tax shortfalls, and prepare 1099 forms if needed. File by April 15 (or March 15 for S-Corps).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the year-end tax preparation checklist for Amazon FBA? ▼

Key steps: 1) Reconcile all accounts (bank, Amazon, credit cards), 2) Complete physical/virtual inventory count, 3) Calculate final COGS, 4) Gather all receipts and expense documentation, 5) Review all deductions, 6) Issue/collect 1099s, 7) Calculate self-employment tax, 8) Prepare and file returns.

When are tax filing deadlines for Amazon FBA sellers? ▼

Sole proprietors and single-member LLCs: April 15 (Form 1040 Schedule C). S-Corps and Partnerships: March 15 (Form 1120S or 1065). Extensions available: October 15 for individuals, September 15 for S-Corps. Quarterly estimates due April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15.

How do I value my ending inventory for taxes? ▼

Count all inventory (at Amazon FBA, in transit, at home/warehouse) as of December 31. Value using your chosen method (FIFO recommended). Include all landed costs. Your ending inventory becomes next year's beginning inventory. Accurate inventory valuation is critical for correct COGS.

What tax forms do Amazon FBA sellers need to file? ▼

Common forms: Schedule C (sole proprietor), Form 1120S (S-Corp), Schedule SE (self-employment tax), Form 8829 (home office), Form 4562 (depreciation), state income tax returns, and sales tax returns. You'll receive Form 1099-K from Amazon for gross sales.
