⚡ Quick Answer

Reduce FBA storage fees by maintaining healthy inventory levels (30-60 days of supply), removing slow-moving inventory before long-term storage fee deadlines, using FBA inventory placement service strategically, and monitoring your IPI (Inventory Performance Index) score above 400.

Overview

Strategies and best practices for minimizing Amazon FBA monthly storage fees and avoiding costly long-term storage fee surcharges.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are Amazon FBA storage fees calculated? ▼

FBA storage fees are charged monthly based on daily average volume (cubic feet) of inventory. Standard-size: $0.87/cubic ft (Jan-Sep) and $2.40/cubic ft (Oct-Dec). Oversize: $0.56/cubic ft (Jan-Sep) and $1.40/cubic ft (Oct-Dec). Plus aged inventory surcharges for items over 181 days.

What is the long-term storage fee penalty? ▼

Amazon charges aged inventory surcharges: $1.50/cubic ft or $0.50/unit (whichever is greater) for inventory 181-365 days old, and $6.90/cubic ft or $0.15/unit for inventory 365+ days old. These are in addition to monthly storage fees.

How do I avoid long-term storage fees? ▼

Create removal orders before the monthly inventory cleanup date (15th of each month). Set up automated removal of aged inventory. Run promotions or lower prices on slow-moving items. Maintain 30-60 days of inventory supply. Monitor the FBA Inventory Age report.

What is a good Inventory Performance Index (IPI) score? ▼

An IPI score above 400 is considered healthy and avoids storage limits. Amazon measures excess inventory, sell-through rate, stranded inventory, and in-stock rate. Focus on reducing excess inventory and fixing stranded listings to improve your score.
