⚡ Quick Answer

Amazon collects and remits sales tax in all states with sales tax laws as a marketplace facilitator. However, FBA sellers still need to file returns in states where they have nexus, track exempt sales, and handle any non-Amazon sales channels. Consult a tax professional for state-specific obligations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Amazon collect sales tax for FBA sellers? ▼

Yes, Amazon collects and remits sales tax on behalf of sellers in all 45 states (plus DC) that have sales tax, under marketplace facilitator laws. However, sellers may still need to file returns in states where they have nexus through FBA inventory storage.

What is sales tax nexus for Amazon FBA sellers? ▼

Sales tax nexus means having a sufficient business connection in a state to require sales tax collection. For FBA sellers, storing inventory in Amazon warehouses creates physical nexus in those states, even though Amazon handles collection.

Do I still need to file sales tax returns if Amazon collects? ▼

Potentially yes. While Amazon collects and remits the tax, many states still require sellers with nexus to file returns (even zero-dollar returns). Check each state's specific requirements or use a service like TaxJar for automated filing.

How do I track sales tax for my Amazon FBA business? ▼

Use the Amazon Tax Document Library for transaction-level sales tax data. Integrate this with accounting software or a sales tax automation service. Keep records of all states where you have inventory stored for nexus tracking.
